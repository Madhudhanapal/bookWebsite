Featured Books

Hamlet, William shakespeare.

The story follows Prince Hamlet, the protagonist, who is deeply affected by the sudden death of his father, King Hamlet. Soon after, his mother, Queen Gertrude, marries his uncle, Claudius, who has ascended the throne.

The Stranger, Albert Camus.

The novel is divided into two parts: the first part follows Meursault’s everyday life, highlighting his passive and indifferent attitude toward the world. When his mother dies, he shows little grief, attending her funeral almost out of obligation.

The Shining, Stephen King.

The Shining (1977) by Stephen King is a psychological horror novel that explores themes of isolation, madness, and supernatural forces. It is one of King’s most famous works and has left a lasting impact on both literature and popular culture.

The Fellowship of the Ring ,J.R.R. Tolkien.

The Fellowship of the Ring (1954) is the first volume in The Lord of the Rings trilogy by J.R.R. Tolkien, a cornerstone of fantasy literature. Set in the richly detailed world of Middle-earth, the novel follows a group of diverse characters on an epic journey to destroy a powerful and evil artifact known as the One Ring.

The Diary of a Young Girl, Anne Frank.

The Diary of a Young Girl by Anne Frank, first published in 1947, is a deeply moving and poignant account of the life of Anne Frank, a Jewish teenager, during the Holocaust. The book is composed of entries from Anne’s personal diary, which she kept while hiding from the Nazis during World War II. It is one of the most widely read and significant works to emerge from the Holocaust, providing a personal and relatable perspective on the horrors of that time.

Silent Spring, Rachel Carson.

Silent Spring by Rachel Carson, published in 1962, is a groundbreaking work that is often credited with launching the modern environmental movement. In this influential book, Carson addresses the dangers of pesticides, particularly DDT, and their detrimental effects on the environment, wildlife, and human health.

The Omnivore’s Dilemma, Michael Pollan.

The Omnivore’s Dilemma: A Natural History of Four Meals by Michael Pollan, published in 2006, is a thought-provoking exploration of the food we eat, the systems that produce it, and the complex choices we face as omnivores. Pollan investigates the origins of our meals and the environmental, ethical, and health implications of our dietary decisions.

The Road ,Cormac McCarthy.

The Road by Cormac McCarthy, published in 2006, is a haunting and poignant post-apocalyptic novel that explores themes of survival, love, and the struggle for humanity in the face of overwhelming desolation. Set in a bleak and devastated world, the story follows a father and his young son as they journey through a landscape stripped of life, civilization, and hope.

The Things They Carried,Tim O’Brien.

The Things They Carried by Tim O’Brien, published in 1990, is a powerful collection of interconnected short stories that explore the experiences of soldiers during the Vietnam War. Blending elements of fiction and memoir, O’Brien delves into the psychological and emotional burdens carried by soldiers, as well as the complexities of memory, storytelling, and the nature of truth in the context of war.
